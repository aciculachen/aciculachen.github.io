Kevin M. Chen

Graduate Research Assistant, OSU ECE

Hello, my name is Kevin Michael Chen, as known as "Acicula" Chen, or Chen Chi-Wen (hanzi:陳啟文). I am currently a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at The Ohio State University, advised by Prof. Parinaz Naghizadeh and co-advised by Prof. Aylin Yener. My research focuses on machine learning, wireless communication, and IoT applications. Prior to my PhD study, I had three years of research experience in machine learning and wireless network communication as a fully-time research assistant advised by Dr. Ronlad Y. Chang at Research Center for Information Technology Innovation (CITI), Academia Sinica, Taiwan.

Apart from the academic activity, I am an amateur guitar player having 10 more years of self-practicing guitar. The guiter hero who inspires and influences me the most is American singer-sonwriter John mayer. I study heavily on his early acoustic songs, like his debut EP Inside Wants Out and the first studio album Room for Squares. Also, I am proud of having his signature Martin guitar, the Martin OM28 JM. Check out here for my JM covers.

Guitars Collection
2021-
Martin OM-28 John Mayer

This guitar was luckily found and purchased through a friend (also a big fan of John Mayer) working in a guitar shop in Taipei, Taiwan. The guitar is the first official John Mayer Signature model made by Martin and was produced as a limited run of only 404, and I own the #173. The guitar features a solid Engelmann spruce top and solid East Indian rosewood back and sides.

This guitar was purchased for celebrating my 20-year-old birthday and is the one that introduces me to Martin Guitar. The guitar features a solid Sitka spruce top and solid East Indian rosewood back and sides.
